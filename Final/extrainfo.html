Genres

- Metal -

A genre of music characterized by distorted electric guitars, drums, and changes in tempo. Countless sub-genre and mixtures of genre have emerged since heavy metals debut in the 60s and 70s.


- Death Metal -

A larger sub-genre of heavy metal. The vocal style tends to be in the lower range, featuring growls instead of highs. Blast beats and tremolo picking are common instrunmental techniques. Song structures are complex and tempo changes often.


- Black Metal -

A larger sub-genre of heavy metal. Similar to Death Metal, Black Metal uses blast beats and tremolo picking. The tempo of black metal tends to be on the faster side and unlike death metal, the vocal style tends to be high in pitch. Another staple cue of black metal is the raw sounding recording and mixing.


- Metalcore -

A sub-genre that infuses elements of melodic death metal and hardcore/punk. This genre is classified by its hardcore-like breakdowns paired with intsrumental melodies and clean and/or screams and growl-like vocals.


- Deathcore -

A sub-genre fusion of death metal and hardcore/punk. It fuses the structure and guitar riffs from death metal with the breakdowns from hardcore. Unlike metalcore, clean singing is not common and death metal blast beats are used often.


- Hardcore -

A sub-culture and sub-genre of punk rock. Rock is not metal, however hardore has many influences in many metal sub-genres. It is similar to the rock genre, but is typically faster and more aggressive in nature.


- Progressive -

A sub-genre that combines the complex structures and time signatures present in progressive rock with the instrumentation of heavy metal. The vocal styles usually lean towards melodic cleans.


- Downtempo -

Like the name suggests, this can be added to other genres to further define a band or song's sound. For example, downtempo deathcore is similar to deathcore, however, as the song progresses, the tempo typically slows down.
